Alan Kay

Computer Scientist and Creator of Smalltalk

About

Alan Kay is an American computer scientist known for his work on object-oriented programming and the development of the Smalltalk programming language.

  • Known for: Smalltalk, object-oriented programming
  • Born: November 17, 1940, Springfield, Massachusetts, USA
  • Nationality: American

Early Life and Education

Kay was born on November 17, 1940, in Springfield, Massachusetts, USA. He received his Bachelor's degree in Molecular Biology from the University of Colorado at Boulder in 1966. He then went on to earn his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Utah in 1969.

Year Education
1966 Bachelor's degree in Molecular Biology, University of Colorado at Boulder
1969 Ph.D. in Computer Science, University of Utah

Career

Kay is best known for his work on the Smalltalk programming language, which he developed in the 1970s at Xerox PARC. He is also known for his contributions to the development of object-oriented programming.

  1. 1970s: Developed Smalltalk at Xerox PARC
  2. 1980s: Worked at Apple Computer
  3. 1990s: Founded Viewpoints Research Institute

Awards and Honors

Kay has received numerous awards and honors for his work, including the Turing Award in 2003.

  • 2003: Turing Award
  • 2001: Kyoto Prize in Advanced Technology
  • 1996: ACM Fellow
